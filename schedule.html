Saturday, October 20, 2012, "Family Day"

Time Event Location
9:00 a.m. – 5:00 p.m. Art Show All art was submitted by Des Plaines residents. Make sure you vote for your favorite in each category. Winners will be announced at the Awards Ceremony following the Sunday performances.
  • 2nd Floor – Youth Entries
  • 3rd Floor – Teen and Adult Entries
9:00 a.m. – 5:00 p.m. An Historical Look at the Arts in Des Plaines Exhibit by the Des Plaines History Center. 1st Floor, Lobby
10:00 a.m. – 2:00 p.m. Community Arts Mural Participate in painting an arts inspired mural on the front window of the library. Drop by any time during the designated hours and let your creative spirit soar. Rhonda Popko of the Des Plaines Art Guild will supervise the project. Library Plaza
10:30 – 11:30 a.m. Scratchboard Drawing Use different tools to create a scratchboard drawing. Kathy Passi of the Des Plaines Art Guild will demonstrate the process and offer hands-on help. 2nd Floor, Storytime Room
10:30 – 11:15 a.m. Storytelling Storyteller Christy Piszkiewicz has a magic pocket in her storytelling apron with lots of story ideas. Dig in and Christy will share a favorite story with you. 2nd Floor, Poet Tree Stage
10:30 – 11:30 a.m. Drumming Drumming is a part of all cultures and musician Jim Chrzan will demonstrate using his collection of drums, bells and other percussion instruments from around the world. Following the demonstration, children and parents will have a hands-on opportunity to try out the various instruments. 2nd Floor, Poetry Alcove
11:00 a.m. – 12:00 p.m. Dances of India Anjali Nrutha Kalalayam will perform dances of India. A Bollywood dance lesson will follow the performance. 1st Floor, Room B/C
12:15 p.m. Mural Unveiling A special mural, the work of Des Plaines schoolchildren, will be unveiled. Under the direction of local artist Janet Thompson, each child drew an outline, and then used acrylic paints on a 12x12 canvas board to create a vision of what Des Plaines means to them. The forty pieces form the mural that will be on permanent display at the library. Meeting Room Hallway
12:30 – 1:30 p.m. Printmaking Basics Learn how to create stamps and relief blocks for use in printmaking. Ed Dougal of the Des Plaines Art Guild will demonstrate and offer hands-on instruction. 2nd Floor, Storytime Room
12:30 – 1:30 p.m. Mexican Folkloric Dance Sol Azteca will perform dances of Mexico. A dance lesson will follow the performance. 1st Floor, Room B/C
1:00 – 1:30 p.m. Old-Time American Music The Destiny Ramblers will perform with accompaniment on banjo and ukulele. 2nd Floor, Poet Tree Stage
1:00 – 3:00 p.m. Playing with Poetry Children, moms and dads are invited to explore the fun, diversity and creativity of famous poets. Toni Rowitz, retired Oakton Community College professor, leads this interactive workshop that concludes with everyone writing together, to create one special poem. 2nd Floor, Poetry Alcove
1:45 – 2:45 p.m. Irish Dance The Dillon-Gavin School of Irish Dance will perform dances of Ireland. A dance lesson will follow the performance. 1st Floor, Room B/C
2:00 – 3:00 p.m. Watercolor Painting Paint a picture of a pumpkin in watercolor. Kristine Plum of the Des Plaines Art Guild will demonstrate and offer hands-on help. 2nd Floor, Storytime Room
2:00 – 4:00 p.m. Amazing Photography with your iPhone/iPad Jerry Hug of the Des Plaines Camera Club will demonstrate iPhoneography. Bring your iPhone/iPad for hands-on help using popular photo apps like AutoStitch and Snapseed. 2nd Floor, Atrium Area
3:00 – 4:00 p.m. The Art of Street Dance Professional dancer Richard Ashworth performs hip-hop dance. Hip-hop had its beginnings in the streets of New York more than twenty-five years ago. Richard has participated in hip-hop battles and has busked (street performed) on street corners around the world. A lesson in street dance will follow the performance. 1st Floor, Room B/C
3:30 – 4:30 p.m. Painting on Silk Join in on painting an arts inspired silk scarf. Rhonda Popko of the Des Plaines Art Guild will demonstrate and offer hands- on assistance. 2nd Floor, Storytime Room
4:00 – 4:30 p.m. Instrumental Guitar Seiji Furukawa will play a selection of music including progressive rock, blues and his own compositions. 2nd Floor, Poetry Alcove
